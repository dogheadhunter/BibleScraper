then let them serve as ministers, as they are free from accusation. + 11 Women should likewise be serious, not slanderous, + moderate in habits, faithful in all things. + 12 Let ministerial servants be husbands of one wife, presiding in a fine manner over their children and their own households. 13 For the men who minister in a fine manner are acquiring for themselves a fine standing and great freeness of speech in the faith that is in Christ Jesus. 14 I am writing you these things, though I am hoping to come to you shortly, 15 but in case I am delayed, so that you may know how you ought to conduct yourself in God’s household, + which is the congregation of the living God, a pillar and support of the truth. 16 Indeed, the sacred secret of this godly devotion is admittedly great: ‘He was made manifest in flesh, + was declared righteous in spirit, + appeared to angels, + was preached about among nations, + was believed upon in the world, + was received up in glory.’ Footnotes ^ Or “have good judgment; be sensible.” ^ Or “a smiter.” ^ Or “managing.” ^ Or “manage.” ^ Or “disgrace.” ^ Or “a good reputation.” ^ Or “not deceitful in speech.” ^ Or “tested as to whether they qualify.” Previous Next Print Share Share
